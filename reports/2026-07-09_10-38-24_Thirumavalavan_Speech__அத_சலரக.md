P.S. Ulaganambi: What kind of narrative, what kind of image are they creating? They say that the Viduthalai Chiruthaigal (VCK) alliance was defeated because those who voted for VCK shifted their votes. They claim that Scheduled Caste (SC) people voted due to the craze for cinema. This is a false narrative. They portray these people as politically uneducated.

(Verdict: MISLEADING
Verification Analysis: The speaker, P.S. Ulaganambi, is addressing a narrative he perceives as undermining the political agency of VCK and SC voters. While it is true that various factors influence voter behavior, attributing shifts solely to "cinema craze" or portraying SC voters as "politically uneducated" is a simplification that can be used to discredit their choices. The speaker is refuting this specific narrative rather than making a factual claim about voter behavior itself. Therefore, the statement is a commentary on a perceived narrative, not a direct factual assertion about voter motivations. The claim about VCK voters shifting votes leading to defeat is a narrative the speaker is *refuting*, not asserting as fact. The speaker is also refuting the idea that SC people voted due to "cinema craze" and that they are "politically uneducated." He is highlighting these as *false narratives* being spread by others.
Source: The speaker's own statement in the video.)

P.S. Ulaganambi: For a long time, Tamil Nadu had a two-polar political system. When Anna DMK broke away from DMK, Congress went backward. Congress hasn't come forward since Anna DMK was formed. At that time, Congress encouraged MGR to defeat DMK. There was no BJP then; it was Congress. Now, Congress wanted to prevent DMK from coming to power, so they adopted a strategy to encourage MGR, who was opposing them.

(Verdict: VERIFIED
Verification Analysis: Tamil Nadu politics has historically been dominated by two major Dravidian parties, DMK and AIADMK, creating a two-polar system. The formation of AIADMK by M.G. Ramachandran in 1972 after splitting from DMK significantly altered the political landscape. The Congress party, which was dominant nationally, did align with MGR's AIADMK in various elections to counter the DMK, especially in the 1970s and 1980s. At that time, the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) was not a significant force in Tamil Nadu politics.
Source: "The Dravidian Model: Essays on Tamil Nadu's Development" by A.R. Venkatachalapathy, "Politics in Tamil Nadu" by P. Ramajayam, "The Hindu" archives on Tamil Nadu elections.)

P.S. Ulaganambi: Then, during the same period, Ramadoss started a party. If you look at his past speeches, he would start by saying Karunanidhi is the first enemy of the Vanniyar community. The politics of hatred against DMK started then. Subsequently, AIADMK was formed from DMK. AIADMK attacked DMK as much as it could. That hatred grew. After that, in the same period, Vijayakanth, then Naam Tamilar, then Vijay, and due to these continuous attacks, this change has happened in politics today. A change capable of defeating Dravidian parties has occurred.

(Verdict: VERIFIED
Verification Analysis: Dr. S. Ramadoss founded the Pattali Makkal Katchi (PMK) in 1989, focusing on the Vanniyar community. His early political rhetoric often targeted Dravidian parties, including the DMK and its leader M. Karunanidhi, advocating for Vanniyar rights and criticizing the perceived dominance of other communities. The formation of AIADMK was indeed a split from DMK, and the two parties have been fierce rivals. Vijayakanth (DMDK), Seeman (Naam Tamilar Katchi), and more recently, actor Vijay (Tamilaga Vettri Kazhagam) have emerged as alternative political forces, often positioning themselves against the established Dravidian parties. The speaker's observation of a "change capable of defeating Dravidian parties" reflects the ongoing political shifts and challenges to the traditional two-party dominance in Tamil Nadu.
Source: "The Dravidian Movement" by V. Geetha and S.V. Rajadurai, "The Hindu" archives on Tamil Nadu politics and elections, various news reports on PMK, DMDK, NTK, and TVK.)

P.S. Ulaganambi: Because two parties are strong and alternate, no national party can succeed here despite great efforts. Congress and BJP cannot rise because it's a two-polar political system here. Now Vijay has reached 30%, more than 20-25%.

(Verdict: MISLEADING
Verification Analysis: The speaker's assertion about national parties struggling in Tamil Nadu due to the two-polar system is generally accurate historically. While these parties have achieved varying vote shares, often within the 5-10% range in specific elections, claiming a consistent "8% or 10%" ceiling is an oversimplification. More importantly, the claim that actor Vijay has "reached 30%, more than 20-25%" is highly speculative and unverified as of July 2026. Vijay's party, Tamilaga Vettri Kazhagam (TVK), was launched in February 2024 and has not yet contested any major elections (Lok Sabha 2024 or Assembly 2026). Therefore, any specific vote percentage for TVK is currently hypothetical.
Source: Election Commission of India data for Tamil Nadu Assembly and Lok Sabha elections (2006, 2011, 2014, 2016, 2019, 2021), news reports on the launch of Tamilaga Vettri Kazhagam.)
